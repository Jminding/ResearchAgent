#### IV. Detailed Mechanism Discussions (Pages 15-45)

**A. X-Ray Emission Mechanisms:**
- Accretion disk-corona model with inverse Compton scattering
- Corona heating mechanisms (magnetic reconnection, viscous dissipation)
- Hard X-ray spectral index Γ ~ 1.6-2.2 correlations with accretion rate
- Variability timescales: minutes to days
- Key References: Reynolds & Begelman, Uttley & Cackett, Done et al.

**B. Optical/Ultraviolet Emission:**
- "Big Blue Bump" (BBB) peaking at λ ~ 1100 Å, T ~ 30,000 K
- Broad-line region emission from photoionization
- Disk thickness measurements from velocity ratios
- Time-lag reverberation mapping (τ ∝ λ^4/3)
- Key References: Koratkar & Blaes, Peterson, Netzer

**C. Infrared Emission and Dusty Torus:**
- Dust reprocessing from accretion disk radiation
- Temperature distribution 100-1500 K
- Clumpy torus covering factors f ~ 0.4-0.9
- Silicate feature (9.7 μm) transitions from emission to absorption with inclination
- Wind-driven vs. accretion-supplied torus models
- Key References: Nenkova et al., Hönig & Kishimoto, Prieto et al.

**D. Radio Emission and Jets:**
- VLBI milliarcsecond resolution: 0.1-2 mas = 0.2-10 pc
- Core-jet morphologies in 90% of sources
- Superluminal motion via Doppler beaming: β_app ~ 1-20c
- Jet kinetic power: 10^43-10^47 erg/s for FRII sources
- Radio largely independent of accretion disk state
- Key References: Zensus, Marscher, Tadhunter

**E. Spectral Energy Distribution (SED) Properties:**
- SED shape primarily controlled by Eddington ratio (λ_Edd)
- Secondary dependence on black hole mass
- Bolometric corrections vary 10-100× with λ_Edd
- UV 1450 Å provides most reliable bolometric proxy
- SED fitting scatter: RMS 0.5-1.0 dex in L_bol estimates
- Key References: Elvis et al., Assef et al., Calistro Rivera et al.

#### V. AGN Diagnostic Indicators (Pages 45-75)

**A. Bolometric Luminosity and Eddington Ratio:**
- Accretion rate: ṁ = λ_Edd × M_BH × c²
- Typical ranges: λ_Edd from 10^-5 (ADAF) to 10^1 (super-Eddington)
- X-ray bolometric corrections: C_X ~ 10-500 depending on λ_Edd
- Step-change in C_X at λ_Edd ~ 0.1 indicates accretion state transition
- UV (1450 Å) C_1450 ~ 5-10 (least variable with AGN properties)
- Key References: Jin et al., Ricci et al., Marconi et al.

**B. Emission Line Diagnostics:**
- BPT diagram: [OIII]/Hβ vs. [NII]/Hα classification
- Seyfert 1 (broad lines), Seyfert 2 (narrow lines), LINER (low ionization)
- Ionization parameter U ranges 10^-2 to 10^-3.5
- [OIII] luminosity correlates with AGN power; can estimate L_bol from [OIII]
- Key References: Baldwin et al., Netzer, Zakamska et al.

**C. X-Ray Spectral Properties:**
- Hard X-ray photon index Γ correlated with λ_Edd
- Soft X-ray excess (temperature ~0.1-0.3 keV) in ~50% of sources
- Iron K-alpha line profiles constraining black hole spin
- Equivalent widths: 0.1-1 keV (neutral); >1 keV (Compton-thick)
- Key References: Done et al., Reynolds, Ricci et al.

#### VI. AGN Unification Models (Pages 75-95)

**A. Classical Unification Paradigm:**
- Type 1/Type 2 dichotomy explained by orientation relative to dusty torus
- Torus covering factor f ~ 0.4-0.9 (depends on opening angle)
- Scattered broad lines detected in ~50% of type 2 Seyferts
- Torus opening angle θ_t ~ 20-45 degrees

**B. Torus Properties and Obscuration:**
- Dust covering factor measurements from infrared luminosity
- Gas column densities: N_H ~ 10^20-10^25 cm^-2
- Dust and gas decoupled: different spatial scales and composition
- Compton-thick fraction: ~20% (z<0.1) to ~40% (z~1)

**C. Refinements and Extensions:**
- Type 1 subclasses (BLS1 vs. NLS1)
- Radio-loud AGN unification; jets largely independent of obscuration
- Wavelength-dependent properties table (soft X-ray to radio)
- Remaining unification challenges (high-z, Compton-thick, NLS1s)

## MAJOR RESEARCH SYNTHESIS

### Consensus Findings

1. **Eddington Ratio as Primary SED Driver**
   - Sources: Jin et al. (2012), Ricci et al. (2017), multiple SED papers
   - Quantitative: SED peak frequency shifts to higher values as λ_Edd increases
   - Bolometric correction C_bol varies 1-2 orders of magnitude with λ_Edd

2. **Torus Covering Factor Independent of Luminosity**
   - Sources: Infrared studies (Nenkova, Hönig, Prieto)
   - Quantitative: f ~ 0.4-0.9; no strong L_bol dependence
   - Size scales with luminosity (r_torus ∝ L^0.5)

3. **Dust and Gas Obscuration Decoupled**
   - Sources: Bianchi (2012), Maiolino & Risaliti (2007)
   - Quantitative: (N_H/A_V) varies 10-100× Galactic value
   - Different spatial scales; disk-sourced gas vs. torus dust

4. **Compton-Thick AGN Significant Population**
   - Sources: Comastri, Ricci et al., obscured AGN surveys
   - Quantitative: ~20-50% of AGN population
   - Critical for cosmic X-ray background understanding

5. **Multi-Wavelength SED Fitting Essential for AGN Characterization**
   - Sources: Elvis et al., Assef et al., Calistro Rivera et al.
   - Quantitative: RMS scatter ~0.5-1 dex in bolometric luminosity without complete coverage
   - Advantages demonstrated in COSMOS, GOODS-South, XMM-SERVS

### Active Debates and Uncertainties

1. **Corona Heating Mechanism**
   - Proposed: Magnetic reconnection vs. viscous dissipation
   - Status: No consensus; both mechanisms may contribute
   - Critical observation: Detailed X-ray spectroscopy could constrain

2. **Radio-Loud/Quiet Dichotomy Origin**
   - Proposed: Black hole spin vs. accretion rate vs. phase dependence
   - Status: Evidence for multiple mechanisms; recent radio-quiet jets discovered
   - Critical observation: Systematic radio surveys; time-domain monitoring

3. **AGN Feedback Efficiency**
   - Proposed: Ranges from 0.1% to 100% of AGN power in different models
   - Status: Observational constraints improving (kiloparsec-scale outflows detected in 50%+ of AGN)
   - Critical observation: Spatially-resolved kinematics; long-baseline monitoring

4. **UV/Optical Continuum Origin**
   - Proposed: Standard thin disk, cloud reprocessing, warm corona, disk-corona interaction
   - Status: No single model fully reproduces observations
   - Critical observation: High-resolution UV spectroscopy; UV/X-ray timing correlations

5. **High-z Black Hole Growth Pathway**
   - Proposed: Rapid early assembly vs. gradual hierarchical growth
   - Status: JWST discoveries of abundant high-z obscured AGN changing consensus
   - Critical observation: Complete AGN census at z>4; black hole mass-stellar mass relationship
