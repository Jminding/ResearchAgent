# AGN Multi-Wavelength Literature: Summary of Key Papers

## Quick Reference Guide to Major Studies (2020-2025)

This document provides citations and abstracts of the most impactful recent papers on AGN multi-wavelength radiation characteristics.

---

## TIER 1: Foundational Recent Studies (2024-2025)

### 1. Warm Corona and Soft X-Ray Excess
**Title**: "Unveiling energy pathways in AGN accretion flows with the warm corona model for the soft excess"
**Citation**: MNRAS 530, 1603 (2024)
**Impact**: Establishes warm corona as physical explanation for ubiquitous soft X-ray excess; ~50% accretion power dissipation
**Key Results**: T = 0.1-1 keV, τ = 10-40, dissipative heating mechanism
**Link**: https://academic.oup.com/mnras/article/530/2/1603/7640859

### 2. Accretion Disk Instability Confirmation
**Title**: "Systematic collapse of the accretion disc in AGN confirmed by UV photometry and broad line spectra"
**Citation**: MNRAS 538, 121 (2024)
**Impact**: First direct observational confirmation of disk instability theory using UV photometry
**Key Results**: Disk collapse at η ~ 0.01; blue continuum drops by factor 2-5
**Link**: https://academic.oup.com/mnras/article/538/1/121/8045605

### 3. Near-Infrared AGN Diagnostics
**Title**: "Near-infrared emission line diagnostics for AGN from the local Universe to z ~ 3"
**Citation**: A&A 678, A147 (2023)
**Impact**: Develops NIR diagnostic lines for dusty AGN; ~60% improvement over optical
**Key Results**: Hidden AGN detection; better high-z applicability with JWST
**Link**: https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2023/11/aa47190-23/aa47190-23.html

### 4. Radio-to-X-Ray SED Fitting
**Title**: "AGNFITTER-RX: Modeling the radio-to-X-ray spectral energy distributions of AGNs"
**Citation**: A&A 688, A112 (2024)
**Impact**: Comprehensive SED fitting code spanning 8 orders of magnitude in frequency
**Key Results**: Simultaneous accretion disk, corona, torus, jet component decomposition
**Link**: https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2024/08/aa49329-24/aa49329-24.html

### 5. Jets in Radio-Quiet AGN
**Title**: "A Relativistic Jet in the Radio Quiet AGN Mrk 110"
**Citation**: arXiv:2506.03970 (2025)
**Impact**: Challenges radio-loud/quiet dichotomy; suggests continuous jet parameter space
**Key Results**: Relativistic jets detected in radio-quiet AGN
**Link**: https://arxiv.org/html/2506.03970

## TIER 2: Major Multi-Wavelength and Diagnostic Studies

### 6. AGN at All Wavelengths Overview
**Title**: "Active Galactic Nuclei at All Wavelengths and from All Angles"
**Citation**: Frontiers Astronomy and Space Sciences (2017)
**Impact**: Comprehensive overview of multi-wavelength AGN emission components
**Key Results**: Confirmed multi-component structure across radio-to-gamma-ray spectrum
**Link**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/astronomy-and-space-sciences/articles/10.3389/fspas.2017.00035/full

### 7. X-Ray Coronal Emission Properties
**Title**: "X-ray properties of coronal emission in radio quiet active galactic nuclei"
**Citation**: Frontiers Astronomy and Space Sciences (2024)
**Impact**: Review of X-ray corona properties in non-jetted AGN
**Key Results**: Hot corona temperature ~100-1000 keV; power-law photon index Γ ~ 1.7-2.5
**Link**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/astronomy-and-space-sciences/articles/10.3389/fspas.2024.1530392/full

### 8. Two-Temperature Accretion Disk Coronae
**Title**: "Local models of two-temperature accretion disc coronae – I. Structure, outflows, and energetics"
**Citation**: MNRAS 527, 2895 (2024)
**Impact**: Theoretical models of disk-corona structure with realistic thermodynamics
**Key Results**: Hot and warm temperature components from energy balance
**Link**: https://academic.oup.com/mnras/article/527/2/2895/7331451

### 9. Dissipative Warm Corona
**Title**: "X-ray view of dissipative warm corona in active galactic nuclei"
**Citation**: A&A 681, A145 (2024)
**Impact**: Energetics and heating efficiency of warm corona
**Key Results**: Accretion power dissipation ~50% in warm corona
**Link**: https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2024/10/aa50111-24/aa50111-24.html

### 10. AGN Variability and UV-X-Ray Disconnect
**Title**: "What drives the variability in AGN? Explaining the UV-Xray disconnect through propagating fluctuations"
**Citation**: MNRAS 530, 4850 (2024)
**Impact**: Reveals weakness in simple X-ray reverberation model
**Key Results**: UV-X-ray correlation coefficient ρ ~ 0.3-0.6 (weak); requires new physics
**Link**: https://academic.oup.com/mnras/article/530/4/4850/7663594

## TIER 3: Observational and Diagnostic Tools

### 11. Black Hole Spin via X-Ray Reflection
**Title**: "Measuring Black Hole Spin using X-Ray Reflection Spectroscopy"
**Citation**: arXiv:1302.3260 (2013); Space Science Reviews 183, 277 (2014)
**Impact**: Established method for measuring supermassive black hole spins
**Key Results**: ~40 AGN with spin measurements; population appears high-spin biased
**Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/1302.3260

### 12. AGN Obscuration and Unified Model
**Title**: "AGN Obscuration and the Unified Model"
**Citation**: arXiv:1201.2119 (2012); Advances in Astronomy (2012)
**Impact**: Validates unified model with dust obscuration explanation
**Key Results**: Column density N_H > 10²² cm⁻² explains Type 1/Type 2 dichotomy
**Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/1201.2119

### 13. WISE Mid-Infrared AGN Selection
**Title**: "MID-INFRARED SELECTION OF ACTIVE GALACTIC NUCLEI WITH THE WIDE-FIELD INFRARED SURVEY EXPLORER"
**Citation**: ApJ 753, 30 (2012)
**Impact**: Simple W1-W2 color criterion for efficient all-sky AGN selection
**Key Results**: 61.9 AGN per deg²; 95% reliability; selects both Type 1 and Type 2
**Link**: https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/0004-637X/753/1/30

### 14. Dusty Torus Composition
**Title**: "Towards an observationally motivated AGN dusty torus model – I. Dust chemical composition from the modelling of Spitzer spectra"
**Citation**: MNRAS 531, 1841 (2024)
**Impact**: Observational constraints on torus dust properties and composition
**Key Results**: Silicate features indicate grain size; clumpy torus preferred
**Link**: https://academic.oup.com/mnras/article/531/1/1841/7679133

### 15. Synchrotron and Inverse-Compton in Jets
**Title**: "Synchrotron and inverse-Compton emission from blazar jets – I. A uniform conical jet model"
**Citation**: MNRAS 423, 756 (2012)
**Impact**: Foundational work on jet SED modeling with two-peak structure
**Key Results**: Synchrotron (radio-UV/X-ray) and IC (X-ray-gamma-ray) emission explained
**Link**: https://academic.oup.com/mnras/article/423/1/756/1747479

## TIER 4: Foundational and Methodology Papers

### 16. Broad-Line Region Reverberation Mapping
**Title**: "The Broad-Line Region in Active Galactic Nuclei"
**Citation**: Peterson, B.M., NED Educational Resource
**Impact**: Comprehensive review of BLR reverberation mapping; 60+ black hole mass measurements
**Key Results**: Size-luminosity relation: r_BLR = 32.9 (L₄₅)⁰·⁵³ light-days
**Link**: https://ned.ipac.caltech.edu/level5/Sept16/Peterson/Peterson2.html

### 17. Narrow-Line Region Photoionization
**Title**: "A New Photoionization Model of the Narrow-line Region in Active Galactic Nuclei"
**Citation**: ApJ 954, 175 (2023)
**Impact**: Updated photoionization models for NLR diagnostics
**Key Results**: Density range 10³-10⁶ cm⁻³; velocity 300-1000 km/s
**Link**: https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/1538-4357/acd757

### 18. Relativistic Jets in AGN - Comprehensive Review
**Title**: "Relativistic Jets from Active Galactic Nuclei"
**Citation**: Annual Review of Astronomy and Astrophysics (2018)
**Impact**: Comprehensive review of ~15 years of jet observations
**Key Results**: 10% AGN radio-loud; jet Lorentz factor Γ ~ 5-20
**Link**: https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/10.1146/annurev-astro-081817-051948

### 19. LINER AGN Nature
**Title**: "The AGN Nature of LINER Nuclear Sources"
**Citation**: Frontiers Astronomy and Space Sciences (2017)
**Impact**: Reviews LINER heterogeneity; develops diagnostic tools
**Key Results**: WHAN diagram cleanly separates ionization sources
**Link**: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/astronomy-and-space-sciences/articles/10.3389/fspas.2017.00034/full

### 20. X-Ray Reverberation Mapping
**Title**: "X-ray reverberation modelling of the continuum, optical/UV time-lags in quasars"
**Citation**: A&A 683, A18 (2024)
**Impact**: Models X-ray reverberation heating of accretion disk
**Key Results**: Explains 1-10 day UV/optical lags through direct illumination
**Link**: https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2024/11/aa50652-24/aa50652-24.html

## TIER 5: Supporting and Specialized Studies

### 21. Blazar Classification and TeV Emission
**Title**: "Blazar nature of high-z radio-loud quasars"
**Citation**: A&A 663, A162 (2022)
**Impact**: High-frequency-peaked BL Lacs are TeV emitters
**Key Results**: HBL identification criteria for gamma-ray follow-up
**Link**: https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2022/07/aa43569-22/aa43569-22.html

### 22. Ionized Gas Outflows in Radio-Quiet AGN
**Title**: "What Drives the Ionized Gas Outflows in Radio-Quiet AGN?"
**Citation**: Galaxies 11, 85 (2024)
**Impact**: Reviews outflow mechanisms in non-jetted AGN
**Key Results**: Radiation pressure and accretion disk winds as outflow drivers
**Link**: https://www.mdpi.com/2075-4434/11/4/85

### 23. Soft X-Ray Excess Testing
**Title**: "Testing warm Comptonization models for the origin of the soft X-ray excess in AGNs"
**Citation**: A&A 611, A36 (2018)
**Impact**: Tests warm corona model predictions against observations
**Key Results**: Warm Compton model explains some but not all soft excess cases
**Link**: https://www.aanda.org/articles/aa/full_html/2018/03/aa31580-17/aa31580-17.html

### 24. Thermal Instability in Warm Corona
**Title**: "Thermal instability as a constraint for warm X-ray corona in AGN"
**Citation**: arXiv:2303.17384 (2023)
**Impact**: Constrains warm corona through thermal stability analysis
**Key Results**: Limits on corona optical depth and heating efficiency
**Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17384

### 25. Future UV Photometry Mission
**Title**: "High-cadence observations of galactic nuclei by the future two-band UV-photometry mission QUVIK"
**Citation**: arXiv:2501.19365 (2025)
**Impact**: Proposed mission for high-cadence UV reverberation mapping
**Key Results**: Disk size measurements; temperature profile derivation
**Link**: https://arxiv.org/html/2501.19365

## Data and Survey References

### Multi-Wavelength Surveys
- **SDSS**: Sloan Digital Sky Survey - optical spectroscopy and photometry
- **XMM-Newton**: European Space Agency X-ray observatory - spectra and photometry
- **Chandra**: NASA X-ray observatory - imaging and grating spectroscopy
- **Swift**: NASA X-ray and UV observatory - rapid follow-up and monitoring
- **Spitzer**: NASA infrared observatory - mid-IR photometry and spectroscopy
- **WISE**: All-sky infrared survey - W1, W2, W3, W4 photometry
- **GALEX**: Galaxy Evolution Explorer - FUV and NUV all-sky survey
- **Fermi LAT**: Gamma-ray Large Area Telescope - gamma-ray observations
- **NuSTAR**: Nuclear Spectroscopic Telescope Array - hard X-rays

### Radio Facilities
- **VLBA**: Very Long Baseline Array - sub-arcsecond radio imaging
- **EVN**: European VLBI Network - combined European telescopes
- **VLA**: Very Large Array - multi-frequency radio observations

## Emerging Frontiers (2025+)

### Near-Term (Next 1-2 Years)
1. JWST rest-frame optical spectroscopy of high-z (z ~ 2-3) AGN
2. Continued broad iron line reverberation spin measurements
3. High-resolution X-ray grating observations (XMM-Newton, Chandra)
4. Multi-wavelength variability monitoring campaigns
5. Mm-wavelength VLBI (Event Horizon Telescope precursors)

### Medium-Term (2-5 Years)
1. AXIS mission (Advanced X-ray Imaging Satellite) - improved high-resolution spectroscopy
2. AMXT (All-sky Monitors X-ray Transient) - continuous monitoring
3. Infrared interferometry (VLTI) - torus structure imaging
4. Large AGN samples with uniform multi-wavelength coverage

### Long-Term (5+ Years)
1. Next-generation X-ray missions with improved sensitivity
2. High-precision black hole spin measurements across AGN population
3. Tests of General Relativity in strong-field regime
4. AGN feedback mechanisms and galaxy evolution coupling
5. Multi-messenger observations (gravitational waves, neutrinos, EM)
