# Literature Review: Multi-Wavelength Radiation Characteristics of Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN)

## Overview

This comprehensive literature review synthesizes recent observational and theoretical advances in multi-wavelength AGN studies, covering: accretion disk physics, coronal heating, X-ray spectroscopy, UV/optical emission lines, infrared properties, radio jets, AGN classification schemes, and diagnostic tools for AGN identification across the electromagnetic spectrum.

---

## Chronological Summary of Major Developments

### Classical Framework (Pre-2010s)
- Unified model: All AGN fundamentally similar, differences from viewing angle and dust obscuration
- Type 1 AGN (broad-line, unobscured) vs. Type 2 AGN (narrow-line, obscured by torus)
- Optical diagnostics: BPT diagrams for AGN classification

### X-ray Revolution (2000s-2015)
- XMM-Newton and Chandra high-resolution grating spectroscopy
- Discovery of warm absorber outflows and multi-component absorption
- Evidence for relativistic winds and ionized outflows

### SED Modeling Era (2010s)
- AGNFITTER and similar codes for simultaneous multi-wavelength fitting
- Radio jets, infrared torus, UV/optical disk, X-ray corona modeled together
- WISE mid-IR color selection for efficient AGN identification

### Reverberation Mapping (2015-Present)
- Time-lag analysis across electromagnetic spectrum
- Direct black hole mass measurements
- Constraints on AGN geometry and component sizes

### Recent Advances (2023-2025)
- Warm corona physics (~50% of nearby AGN)
- UV confirmation of disk instability at low Eddington ratios
- Broad iron line reverberation for black hole spin
- Jets detected in "radio-quiet" AGN

## Key Results and Quantitative Findings

### 1. Multi-Component Accretion Engine

**Hot Corona**:
- Temperature: ~100-1000 keV
- Optical depth: ~1 (optically thin)
- Produces hard X-rays via Compton upscattering
- X-ray spectral index: Γ ~ 1.7-2.5

**Warm Corona** (discovered/confirmed 2023-2025):
- Temperature: ~0.1-1 keV
- Optical depth: ~10-40
- Present in ~50% of nearby AGN
- Contributes ~50% of accretion power when present
- Explains soft X-ray excess below 1 keV

**Accretion Disk**:
- UV/optical continuum peak
- UV spectral slope: α_UV ~ -0.5 to -1
- Shows systematic disk collapse at low Eddington ratios (η ~ 0.01)
- Recent GALEX UV photometry confirms disk instability theory

### 2. X-Ray Spectroscopy Diagnostics

**High-Resolution Spectroscopy** (XMM-Newton RGS, Chandra gratings):
- Resolution: λ/Δλ ~ 1000
- Ionized warm absorbers: 30-50% of Type 1 AGN
- Column densities: 10²¹-10²³ cm⁻²
- Outflow velocities: 100-10,000 km/s

**Iron K-Alpha Complex**:
- Narrow component (6.4 keV): distant torus reflection
- Broad component (4-10 keV): inner disk relativistic motion
- FWHM: 10,000-50,000 km/s
- Constrains black hole spin: ~40 AGN measured

**Soft X-Ray Excess**:
- Observed in ~50% of nearby AGN
- Energy range: 0.1-1 keV
- Primary explanation: warm Compton scattering

### 3. UV and Optical Emission

**Broad-Line Region**:
- Size: 0.01-0.1 pc (10-100 light-days)
- Electron density: 10⁸-10⁹ cm⁻³
- Velocity: FWHM 3,000-10,000 km/s
- Reverberation mapping: >60 AGN with black hole mass estimates

**Narrow-Line Region**:
- Size: 100 pc to kiloparsec scale
- Electron density: 10³-10⁶ cm⁻³
- Velocity: FWHM 300-1,000 km/s

**BPT Diagrams** (Optical Diagnostics):
- Emission-line ratios: [OIII]5007/Hβ, [NII]6584/Hα, [SII]6716,6731/Hα
- Distinguish: Star-forming, Seyfert 1/2, LINERs, composite regions
- Kewley et al. (2001, 2006) classification boundaries

**Near-IR Diagnostics** (2023):
- NIR emission-line ratios better in dusty systems
- ~60% more AGN identified in NIR vs. optical
- Identifies "hidden" obscured AGN

**WHAN Diagram**:
- [NII]/Hα vs. Hα equivalent width
- Distinguishes AGN ionization from shocks and post-AGB stars

### 4. Infrared Properties

**Dusty Torus**:
- Re-emits absorbed UV/optical at 10-40 μm
- Dust temperatures: inner 200-1500 K, outer 50-100 K
- Type 2 AGN: ~50% bolometric luminosity in IR
- Type 1 AGN: ~10% bolometric luminosity in IR

**WISE Mid-IR Selection**:
- Criterion: W1 - W2 ≥ 0.8 (Vega)
- Identifies ~61.9 AGN candidates per deg²
- Completeness: 78%, Reliability: 95%
- Selects both Type 1 and Type 2 AGN

**Infrared Reverberation**:
- IR lags optical/UV by months to years
- Time lag proportional to dust distance from nucleus
- Measures torus geometry

### 5. Radio Jets and High-Energy Emission

**Jet Properties**:
- ~10% of AGN are radio-loud
- Lorentz factor: Γ ~ 5-20
- Synchrotron radiation (radio-to-UV/X-ray)
- Inverse-Compton (X-ray to gamma-ray)

**Radio AGN Types**:
- FR I and FR II radio galaxies (morphology)
- BL Lacs: weak emission lines, νF_ν peak IR-X-ray
  - LBL, IBL, HBL (frequency-peaked subtypes)
  - HBL are TeV emitters
- FSRQ: strong emission lines, νF_ν peak ~GeV-TeV

**Recent Discovery** (2024-2025):
- Jets detected in "radio-quiet" AGN (e.g., Mrk 110)
- Suggests continuous jet parameter space
- Challenges radio-loud/quiet dichotomy

### 6. AGN Classification

**Type 1 vs. Type 2**:
- Type 1: broad lines (FWHM 2,000-10,000 km/s), direct view, ~15-20% of AGN
- Type 2: narrow lines only, obscured by torus (NH > 10²² cm⁻²), ~80-85% of AGN

**Seyfert Subtypes**:
- NLSy1: FWHM(Hβ) < 2,000 km/s, rapid variability, high accretion rate
- BLSy1: FWHM(Hβ) > 4,000 km/s, often gamma-ray loud

**LINERs**:
- ~1/3 of nearby galaxies classified as LINER
- Spectra dominated by low-ionization lines
- Ionization sources: AGN, shocks, or post-AGB stars
- WHAN diagram distinguishes ionization mechanisms

**Radio Classification**:
- Radio-loudness: R = f_5GHz/f_optical
- ~10% radio-loud (R > 1), ~90% radio-quiet (R < 1)
- Unified model: blazar or radio galaxy based on jet inclination

## Identified Gaps and Open Problems

1. Soft X-ray excess origin (warm Compton vs. reflection vs. partial covering)
2. UV/X-ray variability disconnect and weak correlation
3. Black hole spin population statistics (limited sample ~40 measurements)
4. Jet launching mechanisms (why only ~10% powerful?)
5. AGN feedback and wind energetics
6. Dusty torus geometry degeneracies
7. High-redshift AGN physics (z ~ 1-3)
8. LINER heterogeneity and true AGN fraction
9. Radio-quiet AGN jets and jet parameter space
10. SED decomposition model uncertainties

---

## State of the Art (2024-2025)

**Key Capabilities**:
- Multi-wavelength simultaneous observations across 8+ orders of magnitude
- High-resolution X-ray spectroscopy (λ/Δλ ~ 1000)
- Reverberation mapping for black hole masses
- SED fitting (AGNFITTER) with multiple components
- Infrared selection (WISE) with ~80% completeness
- VLBI sub-parsec jet imaging
- JWST rest-frame optical spectroscopy of high-z AGN

**Recent Advances**:
- Warm corona recognized in ~50% of nearby AGN
- UV photometry confirms disk instability
- Broad iron line reverberation spin measurements
- Jets discovered in radio-quiet AGN
- Near-IR diagnostics for dusty AGN

**Quantitative Benchmarks**:
- AGN fraction in galaxies: 1-10% at z ~ 0-2
- Black hole mass range: 10⁶-10¹⁰ M_sun
- Eddington ratio range: 0.01 to > 1
- Radio-loud fraction: ~10%
- LINER fraction: ~1/3 of nearby galaxies
