## Research Coverage Summary

### Wavelength Coverage
- **Radio**: Sub-parsec to kiloparsec jets; VLBI observations; synchrotron radiation
- **Infrared**: Dusty torus; dust composition; WISE selection criteria; reverberation mapping
- **Optical**: Broad-line region; narrow-line region; BPT diagnostics; WHAN diagram; Seyfert classification
- **UV**: Accretion disk signature; disk instability; reverberation mapping; GALEX observations
- **X-Ray**: Hot corona; warm corona; soft X-ray excess; reflection spectroscopy; black hole spin measurement
- **Gamma-Ray**: Jets; blazars; inverse-Compton emission; TeV-emitting BL Lacs

**Total Electromagnetic Spectrum Covered**: ~8+ orders of magnitude (10⁹ Hz radio to 10²⁸ Hz gamma-ray)

### AGN Types Covered
- Type 1 (Seyfert 1, unobscured)
- Type 2 (Seyfert 2, obscured)
- Narrow-Line Seyfert 1 (NLSy1)
- Broad-Line Seyfert 1 (BLSy1)
- Quasars (radio-loud and radio-quiet)
- BL Lac objects (LBL, IBL, HBL)
- Flat-Spectrum Radio Quasars (FSRQ)
- Low-Ionization Nuclear Emission-line Regions (LINERs)
- Radio galaxies (FR I and FR II)

### Physics Components Covered
- **Accretion Physics**: Disk structure, two-temperature corona, warm corona, disk instability
- **Coronal Heating**: Magnetic reconnection, turbulent heating, particle acceleration
- **Black Hole Physics**: Spin measurement, relativistic effects, strong-field gravity tests
- **Photoionization**: BLR and NLR emission, line diagnostics, ionization mechanisms
- **Jets and Outflows**: Formation and collimation, relativistic outflows, accretion feedback
- **Dust Physics**: Torus composition, dust grain properties, infrared re-emission

## Quantitative Benchmarks Referenced

### AGN Demographics
- AGN fraction in galaxies: 1-10% (z ~ 0-2)
- Radio-loud AGN fraction: ~10%
- LINER prevalence: ~1/3 of nearby galaxies
- Type 1 vs. Type 2 ratio: 15-20% Type 1, 80-85% Type 2

### Black Hole Mass and Properties
- Black hole mass range: 10⁶-10¹⁰ M_sun
- Eddington ratio range: 0.01 to > 1.0
- Black hole spin measurements: ~40 AGN (a* from 0 to ~0.998)
- Spin population biased toward high spin (tentative)

### Accretion Disk and Corona
- UV peak: λ ~ 1000-10,000 Å
- Hot corona temperature: ~100-1000 keV
- Warm corona (when present): T ~ 0.1-1 keV, τ ~ 10-40
- Warm corona power contribution: ~50% of accretion luminosity
- X-ray spectral index: Γ ~ 1.7-2.5
- Soft X-ray excess frequency: ~50% of nearby AGN

### Emission Lines and Regions
- BLR size: 10-100 light-days (Seyfert 1)
- BLR electron density: 10⁸-10⁹ cm⁻³
- BLR velocity: FWHM 3,000-10,000 km/s
- NLR size: 100-1000+ pc
- NLR electron density: 10³-10⁶ cm⁻³
- NLR velocity: FWHM 300-1,000 km/s

### X-Ray Diagnostics
- Warm absorber incidence: 30-50% Type 1 AGN
- Absorber column density: 10²¹-10²³ cm⁻²
- Outflow velocity: 100-10,000 km/s
- Iron Kα line FWHM: 10,000-50,000 km/s (broad component)

### Infrared Properties
- Torus dust temperature (inner): 200-1500 K
- Torus dust temperature (outer): 50-100 K
- IR peak wavelength: 10-40 μm
- Type 2 IR luminosity: ~50% bolometric
- Type 1 IR luminosity: ~10% bolometric

### Radio Properties
- Radio-loud AGN jet Lorentz factor: Γ ~ 5-20
- Radio core spectral index: α ~ 0.1-0.3 (flat)
- Radio lobe spectral index: α ~ 0.7-1.0 (steep)
- Radio jet power: 10⁴³-10⁴⁶ erg/s

### Diagnostic Tool Performance
- WISE AGN selection: 61.9 per deg², 95% reliability, 78% completeness
- BPT diagram AGN identification: [OIII]/Hβ > 3
- NIR AGN detection improvement: ~60% more than optical
