## Quick Reference Statistics

### Current LEO Debris (August 2024)
- **54,000** objects > 10 cm
- **1.2 million** objects 1–10 cm
- **140 million** objects 1 mm–1 cm
- **27,000** NORAD-tracked objects

### Tracking Capabilities
- **NORAD:** 10 cm threshold (27,000 objects)
- **ESA TIRA radar:** 1–2 cm detection (2 cm at 1,000 km; 1 cm bistatic)
- **ESA optical:** 10–15 cm near-GEO
- **Gap:** 1.2 million untracked 1–10 cm objects

### Critical Altitude Bands
- **550–600 km:** Debris density parity with active satellites (megaconstellation zone)
- **700–900 km:** 10× higher debris than 400 km (historical accumulation)
- **Sun-synchronous:** Exceeds Kessler syndrome critical threshold

### Model Status
- **MASTER-8:** ESA latest (August 2024 epoch)
- **ORDEM 3.2:** NASA latest (2023, Software of Year 2024)
- **Coverage:** 10 μm to 1 m (ORDEM); 1 μm to ∞ (MASTER)
- **Divergence:** Models differ significantly for 1–10 cm uncatalogued debris

### Empirical Data
- **Collision velocity:** 9.7–10.6 km/s
- **Conjunction statistics (2022):** ~260,000 events
- **Fragmentation events (cumulative):** 250+ (as of 2015)
- **2024 major events:** Intelsat 33e (~20K fragments); Long March 6A (700–900 fragments)

### Active Debris Removal Status
- **ADRAS-J:** First inspection mission (July 2024) - successful
- **Clearspace-1:** Planned 2026 removal mission (ESA)
- **Market:** 62% market share for debris removal (2024)
- **Throughput gap:** ADR capacity insufficient for generation rate
