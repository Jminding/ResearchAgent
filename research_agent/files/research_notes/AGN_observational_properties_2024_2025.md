# AGN Observational Properties Across Wavelengths - 2024-2025 Research Notes

## Executive Summary
Recent observations from 2024-2025 using multiple space telescopes (Chandra, Swift, NuSTAR, JWST, Fermi) and ground-based observatories have significantly advanced our understanding of AGN across the electromagnetic spectrum. Key findings include complex X-ray variability patterns, detection of heavily obscured AGN populations, gamma-ray flares on minute timescales, and unprecedented infrared spectroscopy of high-redshift AGN.

---

## 1. X-RAY OBSERVATIONS AND VARIABILITY

### Key X-ray Variability Statistics

- **X-ray power spectrum slope**: -1 (universal across all AGN regardless of luminosity and black hole mass)
- **Variability amplitude decrease**: Decreases with increasing accretion rate (inverse correlation)
- **Anti-correlation detected**: Between power spectral density function amplitude and accretion rate
- **Large amplitude outbursts**: ≥factor 2 on short timescales (~days) occur ~once every 100 days
- **Small amplitude variability**: ~20% on shorter timescales (~1 hour) detectable with sensitive instruments
- **Seyfert spectral properties**: Seyfert I and Seyfert II power spectra are identical (supporting unification models)
- **Redshift trend**: Weak intrinsic average increase of X-ray variability with redshift

### Coronal Emission Properties (December 2024)

- **Electron temperature**: Te ∼ 10^8–10^9 K (thermal Comptonization in corona)
- **Coronal variability types identified**:
  - Stochastic variability
  - Quasi-periodic variability
  - Flares
- **Optical depth (warm corona)**: τ ≈ 10–20
- **Warm corona temperature**: ~1 keV
- **Compton-thick reflector**: log NH,refl/cm⁻² ≈ 24.5 (typical covering factor ~0.5)

### Swift/BAT Survey Results (February 2024)

- **Energy band studied**: 14–195 keV hard X-ray
- **Survey duration**: 157 months
- **Sample size**: Multiple AGN across Seyfert types
- **Key finding**: UV/optical variability more complex than expected from disc reverberation models
- **Temporal correlation**: UV/optical light curves poorly correlated with X-rays
- **Time-scale discrepancy**: Observed timescales longer than theoretical predictions

## 2. OPTICAL AND INFRARED EMISSION

### Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (JWST/NIRSpec - 2023-2024)

- **Hidden AGN detection**: ~60% more AGN revealed in near-IR vs. optical diagnostics
  - Optical sample: 8 AGN (64 sources at z ≤ 1, 65 sources at z = 1–3)
  - Near-IR sample: 13 AGN (from same sources)
  - Hidden AGN classification: 5 sources showing optically thick dust
- **Wavelength advantage**: Near-IR is 10× less obscured than optical
- **Spectral regime**: Rest-frame near-IR (0.8–2.4 μm)
- **Dust attenuation reduction**: Longer wavelengths provide better characterization of intrinsic AGN properties

### "Little Red Dots" (LRDs) Population (2024)

- **Total sample size**: 341 "little red dots"
- **Redshift range**: z ∼ 2–11
- **Survey coverage**: CEERS, PRIMER, JADES, UNCOVER, NGDEEP
- **Redshift distribution**:
  - Density increases at z < 8
  - Rapid decline begins at z ∼ 4.5
- **RUBIES program (2024)**: 60 hours JWST observation time
  - 4,500 distant galaxies observed spectrally
  - 35 little red dots discovered in dataset
- **Primary population characteristics**: 116 LRDs in complete JWST/NIRSpec archive (Jan-Dec 2024)

### Dwarf Galaxy AGN (JWST/NIRSpec 2024-2025)

- **Near-infrared coronal lines detected**: 16 unique species across 4 dwarf galaxies
- **First spatially resolved observations**: Provided unprecedented data on AGN feedback in dwarf galaxies

### MIRI AGN Selection (2024)

- **Total sources analyzed**: 3,273 MIRI-detected sources
- **Survey area**: ~34 arcmin²
- **AGN candidates identified**: 217 total
  - Primary sample (normal massive galaxies, M* > 10^9.5 M☉, z ∼ 0–4): 111 AGN
  - Extended sample (low-mass galaxies, M* < 10^9.5 M☉): 86 AGN candidates
  - High-z sample (z ∼ 4–8.4): 20 AGN candidates
- **Redshift coverage**: ≈0–5

## 3. RADIO JETS AND MORPHOLOGIES

### Global Jet Properties (2024-2025)

- **Jet viewing angles**: 25°–81° (kinematic estimates)
- **Edge-brightened morphology prevalence**: More common than previously thought (Global VLBI Alliance findings)
- **Jet power dependence**: Radio AGN incidence shows black hole kinetic power dependence
- **Morphology classification effects**:
  - Compact vs. complex radio morphologies show different mass-dependence
  - Different kinetic power dependencies identified

### Double-Double Radio Galaxies (2025)

- **Morphological signature**: Two or more pairs of radio lobes on opposite sides of core
- **Physical interpretation**: Indicates period of AGN inactivity followed by reactivation
- **Timescale memory**: Bow shock and cocoon retain memory of jet outburst history on megayear timescales

### High-Redshift Radio AGN Observations (2024-2025)

**4C+03.24 VLA Observations (Dec 2024–Jan 2025)**:
- **Observation details**: Ku-band (12–18 GHz), 5 hours dedicated observation (8.7 hours total)
- **Rest-frame frequency**: 70 GHz
- **Jet structure discovery**: 3 distinct spatial peaks in central jet structures

**4C+19.71 (z = 3.5892) JWST/NIRSpec Observations**:
- **Emission lines detected**: 24 rest-frame optical emission lines
- **Observation type**: Integral field spectroscopy
- **Confirmation**: High-energy (radio jet) evolution evidence in massive galaxies

### NGC 1068 Multi-wavelength Jet Study (2024)

- **Instruments**: e-MERLIN and VLA radio, ALMA
- **Jet lobe observations**: NE jet lobe showing bow shocks coinciding with molecular gas outflows
- **Jet power measurement**: Responsible for driving dense molecular gas (ALMA-detected)
- **Multi-wavelength correlation**: Direct evidence of jet-driven feedback on molecular gas

### Precessing and Flickering Jets

- **Observation characteristics**: Large-angle, short-period precessing jets detected
- **Host galaxies**: Found in dynamically disturbed galaxies
- **VLBA detections**: Evidence of orbital motion in radio-loud AGN
- **Jet variability timescales**: Flickering jets studied over megayear timescales

## 4. GAMMA-RAY ACTIVITY

### Fermi-LAT AGN Survey (2024-2025)

- **Cumulative observation time**: 16 years of operations (as of June 2024)
- **AGN prominence**: Most abundant, most powerful, and persistent gamma-ray emitters in the sky

### 4FGL Catalog Periodicity Study (2024)

- **Sample size**: 1,492 jetted AGN analyzed
- **Observation duration**: 12 years of Fermi-LAT data
- **Largest sample studied**: To date for periodicity analysis
- **Variable AGN identification**: Variability index ≥ 18.48 (4FGL-DR2 catalog)
- **Variable AGN fraction**: 1,620 total variable jetted AGN = 48.9% of initial sample

### Gamma-Ray Variability Timescales

- **Shortest observed GeV timescale**: 4.8 minutes (blazars)
- **Minute-timescale variability**: Detected with Fermi-LAT
- **Orbital timescale variations**: Two-minute binned data shows softer-when-brighter trend (significant positive correlation)
- **Multi-timescale analysis**: Daily, 3-hour, 2-minute, and orbital binned light curves analyzed

### Long-Term Trends (2024)

- **Sources studied**: 1,492 jetted AGN
- **Sources with trends**: 40 AGN exhibited long-term trends
  - Quadratic trends: 8 sources
  - Linear trends: 32 sources

### VHE Gamma-Ray Detections

- **Very-high-energy (VHE) sources**: >100 GeV
- **Misaligned jetted AGN**: 12 sources detected at high significance
- **Observation basis**: 16+ years Fermi-LAT data

### Radio-Quiet AGN Gamma-Ray Detection (2024)

- **Detection significance**: 5.2σ (robust detection)
- **Source type**: Seyfert galaxies (radio-quiet)
- **Observation duration**: 15-year Fermi-LAT observations
- **Physical origin**: Hot coronae gamma-ray emission

## 5. MULTI-WAVELENGTH CORRELATIONS

### Optical/UV-X-ray Relationship (2024)

- **Key finding**: Optical/UV and X-ray emissions NOT as well correlated as expected from models
- **Correlation status**: Weak or absent in some sources
- **Variability structure**: Indicates different physical origins or complex propagation mechanisms

### Continuum Time-Delay Observations (2024-2025)

**HALO (Hubble Constant Constraints through AGN Light curve Observations) Project**:
- **Monitoring period**: September 20, 2024 – February 17, 2025
- **Observation strategy**: Simultaneous inter-band continuum time-delay measurements and SED modeling
- **Luminosity range**: Broad AGN luminosity sample
- **Redshift coverage**: Multiple redshift range
- **Photometric filters**: Strömgren and y filters for optical continuum
- **Minimized contamination**: Spectral regions with minimal emission line contribution

### Changing-Look AGN 1ES 1927+654

- **Campaign duration**: May 2022 – April 2024 (high cadence multi-wavelength observations)
- **Simultaneous event**: Unprecedented radio flare
- **Jet emergence**: Spatially resolved jet detection during observations
- **Timescale**: Months to years (extreme optical, UV, X-ray luminosity variations)
- **Physical cause**: Rapid, extreme accretion disk changes around supermassive black hole

### Dual AGN Studies (2024-2025)

- **Observational approach**: Optical Integral Field Unit spectroscopy + ALMA
- **Target redshift**: z < 0.1 (nearby universe)
- **Physical correlation**: Black hole growth – galaxy evolution connection
- **Sample characteristics**: Merging galaxy systems
- **Companion detection**: JWST + ALMA discovering companions at ≲18 kpc separation

- **Method**: Intensive broadband reverberation mapping
- **Goal**: Infer AGN accretion disk properties through inter-band time lags

**Historical Discovery (2024)**:
- **Detection**: First signal from pair of monster black holes disrupting gas cloud in galaxy center
- **Significance**: Unprecedented multi-wavelength signature of dual AGN interaction

## 8. CORRELATIONS AND PHYSICAL INSIGHTS

### X-ray/Accretion Rate Relationship

- **Inverse correlation**: Variability amplitude decreases with increasing accretion rate
- **Physical mechanism**: Related to accretion disk structure and corona geometry
- **Black hole mass independence**: Power spectral properties independent of black hole mass
- **Binary comparison**: AGN power spectrum consistent with Cyg X-1 soft state (suggesting universal accretion physics)

### UV-Optical-X-ray Connections

- **Temporal lags**: UV/optical variability lags at longer wavelengths
- **Timescale mismatch**: Observed lags exceed theoretical predictions
- **Poor correlation**: Indicates complexity beyond simple disc reverberation
- **Propagating fluctuations**: Recent models invoke inward propagation to explain observations

### Radio/Jet-Driven Molecular Feedback

- **Direct observation**: Jets driving molecular gas outflows (NGC 1068)
- **Quantifiable impact**: Jet power measurements enabling feedback energy calculations
- **Long-term signatures**: Precessing/flickering jets leave morphological memory

### Infrared-Selected AGN Properties

- **Dust obscuration**: Optical classification biased against dusty sources
- **Near-IR advantage**: Reveals hidden AGN population (~60% increase in detection)
- **Mid-IR selection**: Identifies heavily obscured AGN population at high redshift
- **Spectral energy distribution**: SED analysis enables multi-component decomposition

## 10. KEY STATISTICAL FINDINGS

### AGN Population Statistics

- **Variable jetted AGN fraction**: 48.9% (1,620 of ~3,300 in 4FGL-DR2)
- **Hidden AGN increase (near-IR)**: 60% more detections vs. optical
- **Compton-thick AGN**: Significant fraction identified in NuSTAR-NEP survey
- **High-redshift AGN abundance**: Sustained or enhanced at z ≈ 9–11 (JWST findings)

### Variability Parameters

- **X-ray timescale**: Hours to days for small-amplitude (~20%) variability
- **Gamma-ray timescale**: Minutes to years (4.8 minutes to months observed)
- **Radio jet precession**: Megayear timescales
- **Accretion disk response**: Days to weeks (reverberation lags)

### Spectral Properties

- **X-ray power-law slope**: -1 (universal, regardless of AGN type)
- **High-energy cutoff**: Multiple temperature components identified
- **Rest-frame optical emission lines**: Up to 24 detected per source (high-z radio AGN)
